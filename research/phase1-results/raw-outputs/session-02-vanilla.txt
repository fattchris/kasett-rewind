## Incident Summary: JWT Auth Failure — v3.1.0 (ARCHIVED)

### Incident Overview
- **Status:** Resolved and archived
- **Duration:** ~70 minutes
- **Users Affected:** 340
- **Endpoint:** `https://auth.moltaicorp.com/v1/tokens/refresh`

### Root Cause
A regression introduced in **v3.1.0** changed the JWT expiry check in `/opt/services/auth-service/src/middleware/jwt.ts` to compare `Date.now()` (milliseconds) directly against `token.exp` (seconds), causing all tokens to appear immediately expired and returning 401s on every refresh attempt.

### Fix
**Hotfix version:** `v3.1.0-hotfix`
- **Change:** `Date.now() > token.exp` → `Date.now() / 1000 > token.exp`
- **File:** `/opt/services/auth-service/src/middleware/jwt.ts`
- All 12 existing unit tests passed; hotfix deployed and confirmed working via test session `a3f8c201-9d44-4b7e-8c22-1f0e3b5d9a77` (HTTP 200 returned).

### Prevention Measures Added
1. **New unit test:** `'should reject tokens with expiry in milliseconds'` added to `/opt/services/auth-service/src/middleware/jwt.test.ts`
2. **ESLint rule:** Flags direct `Date.now()` comparisons against JWT `exp` without `/1000` division

### Post-Incident
- Postmortem written and sent to team
- Affected users required no manual backfill — natural re-authentication via the now-fixed refresh endpoint
- Endpoint confirmed stable at baseline error rate on `v3.1.0-hotfix`
